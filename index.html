About the Book

Knowledge is only powerful when you can use it.

This is not a book about being naturally gifted. It is a book about being deliberate.

Oluwatobiloba Daniel Abiodun-Oni graduated with a First Class in Social Work, achieving a 5.0 GPA across four consecutive semesters. He did not begin university that way. He began in the wrong course, failed critical exams, spent weeks unable to leave his room, and rebuilt — quietly, strategically, and completely.

How to Get a 5.0 GPA is the method behind that result. Five principles drawn from lived experience, covering every dimension of academic excellence: finding genuine interest in your work, building a relationship with God that extends into your study life, setting goals that are specific and visible, fighting for every mark across every avenue through which marks are earned, and doing more than what is required.

This book is short by design. There is no padding, no filler, no content included simply to add weight. Every principle is actionable and every chapter closes with a concrete step the reader can take immediately. The author's conviction is simple: the information that transforms academic performance should be available to every student, not only those who happen to attend the right seminar or know the right person.

Whether you are failing, surviving, or already performing well — this book was written for the student who has decided that good enough is not enough.

What you'll master:

✓ Why students fail before the semester even starts.
✓ How to build the academic foundation that outlasts motivation.
✓ The goal-setting method with a 95% success rate.
✓ Fight for Every Mark: how to consistently score 90s.
✓ How "doing too much" puts you in the top 1%.

About the Author

Oluwatobiloba Daniel Abiodun-Oni graduated with first-class honors in Social Work and Human Services from Babcock University. He grew up in Olambe, Ogun State, before heading off to Babcock University to study Social Work and Human Services.

He currently works in marketing, where he gets to sit at the intersection of business and creativity. As academic director of his department, he ran support programs that helped struggling students turn their performance around, an experience that later became the spark for this book.

Outside of work, he's passionate about mental health, and you'll usually find him singing or playing the piano when he's not working.

Why I Wrote This Book

"Helping people is one of my loudest characteristics as a person; this book is written to help students, no matter whether they are performing poorly, averagely or well, to achieve excellence. I have been through all the phases of academic success, low performance, average performance and excellence, and I can proudly say excellence is sweet. Why not introduce people to it?"

"The push to write this book came after one of the academic support programmes I held as academic director of my department. That semester, after that event, a lot of people's academic performance improved, and I thought many people missed out on this life-changing information on achieving excellence and decided to make it into a book, making it available to everyone. Also, I had 4 perfect GPA semesters. Who else is more fit to write a book on this? Hahaha"

— Oluwatobiloba
